Texas

Texas is a large and diverse state with a rich history and unique culture. Here are 10 interesting facts about the Lone Star State:

  • Texas is the second largest state in the U.S., covering 268,596 square miles. It’s almost three times the size of the UK!
  • Austin is the capital of Texas, with a population of 965,872 (as of 2020). It’s the 4th most populated city in the state.
  • Houston is the most populous city in Texas, with over 2.3 million people. It’s the 4th largest city in the U.S.
  • The Texas flag is called the "Lone Star Flag." It was designed in 1839, and its red, white, and blue colors represent courage, purity, liberty, and loyalty.
  • The Bluebonnet is Texas' state flower. It blooms from late March to early April and signals the arrival of spring.
  • The Rodeo is the state sport. It includes events like bull riding and barrel racing and was named the state sport in 1997.
  • Chili Con Carne is the state dish of Texas. It’s a spicy beef stew without beans, a dish rooted in cowboy cooking.
  • The Monarch Butterfly is Texas’ state insect. The state is home to more butterfly species than any other, with the Monarch being the most famous
  • The Pecan Tree is the state tree. Pecans are used in many Texan treats, like pecan pie, and are the only commercially grown nut in the state.
  • The Prickly Pear Cactus is the state plant. Its fruit is used in jellies, pies, and even wine, and it was made the state plant in 1995.
